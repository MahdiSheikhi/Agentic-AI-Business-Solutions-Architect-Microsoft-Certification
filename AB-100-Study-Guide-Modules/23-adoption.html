Domain 3 · Deploy

Adoption — Guiding the AI-Forward Organization

The user enablement framework (four phases)

  1. Get ready — enablement team + executive sponsors, an AI council, prioritized scenarios, success criteria/KPIs, readiness assessment.
  2. Onboard & engage — Center of Excellence / Community of Practice, Champions and Early Adopters programs, training + engagement strategy. Champions contextualize value for peers and absorb IT Q&A at scale.
  3. Deliver impact — scenario rollout, measurement against the defined KPIs.
  4. Extend & optimize — agents and extensibility; continuous improvement.

Support kit: Copilot Success Kit, adoption hub templates, workshops.

Measurement instruments (know which measures what)

Instrument What it tells you
Copilot Dashboard (Viva Insights) Licensed vs active users (28-day), adoption by group/app, returning users, benchmarks, Copilot-assisted hours/value — no paid Viva Insights needed
AI adoption score (M365 admin center) 100 = every licensed user active 3 days/week (12 of last 28 days) — the documented habit threshold; peer benchmarks
Copilot adoption report (Viva Insights template) Usage levels: power (≥15 actions/week + ≥75% active weeks) · habitual (consistent, <15/week) · novice (<75% of weeks) — with per-level interventions
Copilot Analytics / Copilot Control System Readiness → adoption → impact, agent usage/billing/success, business-metric uploads (SAP/Salesforce/Workday)

Practices that separate AI-forward orgs (documented guidance)

  • Define target outcomes per role before measuring; baseline task times pre-rollout; keep a non-user comparison group.
  • Treat time-saved as a leading indicator — track where reclaimed time goes (the lagging outcome is what the business buys).
  • Governance hygiene from day one: audit the agent inventory/registry for unowned production agents; enforce named owners, risk-tier classification, and an intake process.
  • Common stalls → fixes: many pilots, no portfolio → tie every agent to an owner + outcome; licenses ≠ usage → systematic role-based enablement; shadow agents → operational governance baseline (inventory, DLP, quarantine).
  • Champion the AI lifecycle inside development too (exam responsibility "promote adoption across the development lifecycle"): Copilot-assisted development, AI-generated test cases, plan designer for envisioning, evaluation gates in CI/CD.

⚡ Exam focus: the 3-days-per-week habit threshold, power/habitual/novice tiers, champions + AI council as the enablement backbone, and "baseline before rollout + comparison group" as the measurement discipline.
